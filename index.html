Where Tradition Meets Excellence

Casita Miramar is more than a restaurant—it's a celebration of Puerto Rican heritage. Founded in 2010 as the sister restaurant to the legendary La Casita Blanca, we continue a culinary tradition that began in 1980.

Housed in a beautifully restored 1920s villa that once served as Hotel Toro, our space is filled with antique furnishings and stories of generations past. Here, every meal is a journey through time, honoring the recipes of grandmother Aurora Ruiz.

From the moment you arrive with our complimentary plantain soup and bacalaítos, to your farewell shot of chichaíto, we ensure every guest experiences the warmth and authenticity of true Puerto Rican hospitality.

Three Generations of Excellence

1980

La Casita Blanca

Jesús Manuel Pérez, inspired by his mother Aurora Ruiz's recipes, opens La Casita Blanca—a restaurant that would become a beloved institution for authentic Puerto Rican cuisine.

2010

Casita Miramar Opens

Leonardo Pérez joins his father to open Casita Miramar in a historic 1920s villa, expanding the family's culinary legacy while maintaining the traditions that made La Casita Blanca special.

2024

James Beard Recognition

La Casita Blanca receives the prestigious James Beard Foundation's "America's Classics Award," cementing the Pérez family's commitment to preserving authentic Puerto Rican culinary traditions.
